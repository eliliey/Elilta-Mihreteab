Disproportionate Air pollution on communities of color

Many of Americans are still breathing unhealthy air, and the burden is not evenly shared. Some communities are disproportionately impacted by air pollution - those facing the greatest burden are certain communities of color and low-income communities. These communites are disproportionately exposed to the most dangerous air pollutants known as PM.

What are the main cause of air pollution?

Carbon emissions like burning fossil fuels such as coal and oil are the main cause of air pollution. The fuels are burned by power plants, factories, and even your car. When burned, these fuels release large amounts of carbon dioxide into the air. Click here for ways to you can take action against air pollution and social justice.

Effects of Air pollution on the communities of color

The effect of air pollution(bad air quality) are overwhelmingly concentrated in communities of color. The lasting implications of segregation and redlining mean that People of Color(Black , Asian, and Hispanic communities) are more likely to live near freeways, oil and gas drilling sites, and other pollutants, and their communities have higher rates of air pollution. Resultantly, people of color experience more chronic diseases such as asthma, heart attacks and strokes, lung diseadse reproductive harm, premature birth and low-birth weight, and even early death.
